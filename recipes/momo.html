Momo

Momo is a type of steamed dumpling with some form of filling. Although Momo is also consumed in Tibet and areas of India, this specific recipe is based on the traditional dish made in Nepal. The key differentiator between the Nepali recipe and others further afield is the dipping sauce. In Tibet, Momo are served with chili garlic sauce or pickled daikon, but in Nepal, theyâ€™re served with a homemade spicy tomato chutney called Achaar.

  1. Add the flour, salt, and water to a bowl. Add more water if it's too dry. Knead for 5 minutes to form an elastic dough. Set aside for 20 minutes in cling film. Roll out the dough into a long sausage shape. Cut the dough into 1 inch chunks. Using a rolling pin, roll out individual wrappers into circles, repeat and set aside. Cover with cling film to avoid the wrappers going dry.
  2. In a bowl, combine the filling ingredients and mix well to form a paste. To fill the Momo, take a wrapper and use 1 to 2 teaspoon of mixture and place it in the centre of the wrapper. Then slowly crease the edge of the wrapper pinching around the filling, until the circle is complete.
  3. Place the dumplings in a steamer and cook for 8 minutes.
  4. Put the Momo on a plate with the dipping sauce, and serve up!
